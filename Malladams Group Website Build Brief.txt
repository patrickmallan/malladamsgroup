### Mission Statement



Malladams Group exists at the intersection of energy, design, and legacy. We believe that 99.9% of existing structures — physical, organizational, and social — were built without energetic integrity at their foundation. The result is spaces and systems that exhaust, deplete, and ultimately collapse. We are building a different way forward. We call it **energetic architecture**: a three-layer framework that ensures every structure we touch — from the land it sits on to the people who move through it — is designed to sustain and amplify life.



### The Core Belief



Energy is the missing link. Every structure that lasts — truly lasts, across generations — was built on energetic integrity, whether its creators knew it or not. We know it. And we build accordingly.



### What Malladams Group Does



We operate on two tracks:



**Development** — We acquire, design, and activate properties using our energetic architecture framework. Our first development is underway in Squamish, British Columbia — a living proof of concept for everything we believe.



**Consultancy** — We guide organizations, developers, entrepreneurs, and visionaries through the same three-layer framework for their own projects. Every development we build becomes a case study. Every system we design becomes a template.

## 03. THE FRAMEWORK — “THE THREE LAYERS”



This is the intellectual core of the brand. It must be presented clearly and beautifully on the site.



### Layer One: Land & Location



*Before a single decision is made.*

We begin where most people never think to begin — with the energetic quality of the land itself. Not every site can hold every vision. We help identify locations and properties that are energetically aligned with the life meant to be lived and built within them. Site selection is not just practical. It is sacred.



### Layer Two: Physical Space



*Form, function, and beauty as one.*

We design physical environments where nothing is arbitrary. Every material, every proportion, every threshold is chosen with intention. We work at the intersection of energetic principles, architectural integrity, and aesthetic beauty — because beauty is not decoration. Beauty is a signal that a space is in right relationship with itself.



### Layer Three: Operating Structures



*How humans move through and within the space.*

A beautiful building with a broken internal culture is still a broken structure. The third layer is how people are hired, how leadership is practiced, how daily protocols are designed — the invisible architecture that either sustains or drains the life force of everyone inside. We design this too.

## 07. VISUAL DIRECTION



### Aesthetic



**Luxurious. Alive. Grounded. Iridescent.**

Think dragon energy — rich depth, warmth, power without aggression. Not cold luxury. Living luxury. The kind of space that exhales when you walk into it.



### Color Palette (suggested — Patrick can refine)



- **Deep forest green** `#1B3A2D` — primary dark tone

- **Warm obsidian** `#1A1410` — backgrounds

- **Burnished gold** `#C9A84C` — accent, hover states, dividers

- **Dusty rose / antique blush** `#C4937A` — secondary accent, warmth

- **Cream / warm white** `#F5EFE6` — body text, light sections

- **Iridescent overlay** — subtle shimmer/gradient layer on hero and section dividers (think oil-on-water, abalone, dragon scale)



### Typography



- **Display / Headlines:** Something with presence and soul — suggested: *Cormorant Garamond* (elegant, historic, architectural) or *Playfair Display* (luxurious, feminine power)

- **Body:** *Jost* or *DM Sans* — clean, modern, highly readable

- **Accent / Pull Quotes:** *Cormorant Garamond Italic* — for atmosphere



### Motion & Feel



- Slow, intentional scroll reveals (fade up, not bounce)

- Subtle parallax on hero section

- Hover states on CTAs: gold glow or gentle shimmer

- No aggressive animations — everything moves like it has weight and intention

- Section transitions: gentle fade, not hard cuts



### Imagery



- **Founders photo:** Family photo (Annissa with family) — background to be replaced with abstract, rich, atmospheric imagery. Deep tones, iridescent quality, not literal (no mountains, no interiors — pure color and light abstraction). Think: deep jewel tones dissolving into each other. Abalone. The inside of a geode.

- **Supporting imagery:** Where stock or generated images are used — natural materials (stone, wood, water, light through glass), architectural details, earth and sky. Nothing corporate. Nothing generic.



### Overall UI Principles



- **Super user friendly is non-negotiable.** Navigation must be effortless. CTAs must be obvious. Mobile must be perfect.

- Generous white space (or dark space) — let things breathe

- Nothing cluttered. Nothing competing.

- Every element earns its place.
